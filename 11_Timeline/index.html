Utah Valley University

2011 - 2014

During this time I was studying biology, working as an academic tutor, and preparing to take the Medical College Admission Test (MCAT). I was also heavily involved in basic laboratory research studying evolution as well as multiple volunteer activities. I graduated in 2014 with a bachelor of science (cum lade) and prepared to start medical school in the summer of 2014 at the University of Utah.

University of Utah School of Medicine

2014 - 2021

Medical school was an incredibly rigorous endeavor to say the least. It required 60-80 hours a week of constant studying, attending classes, and never-ending tests during the pre-clinical years. As a more senior medical student, I spent several years rotating through various medical subspecialties at multiple hospitals and outpatient clinics which typically required a staggering 80-100 hour work weeks. In the end, I crammed a seemingly impossible amount of information into my brain and can now proudly put the distinguished "M.D." after my name.

Physeo

2016 - present

After completing two years of medical school, I decided to take a leave of absence for one year to co-found a company known as Physeo (www.Physeo.com). I successfully raised venture capital, organized a team of co-founders, and began creating educational content for medical students. Over time, our company grew and I decided to take another leave of absence for two years. During this time I hired and directly managed a team of 70 people. Fastforward to today and we have approximately 1,000 videos with a team of individuals who continue to produce educational content. I continue to be minimally involved in the company today.

Vanderbilt University Medical Center

2021 - present

Following medical school, I began medical residency training in clinical pathology. I am currently in my second year of residency with one more year to go. At this point in my training, I spend part of my day managing a clinical laboratory, performing microscopic evaluations of patient tissue, and interpreting laboratory results. My schedule is fairly light and allows me to have free time to pursue other interests (eg, programming).

Developer

2020 - present

My interest in programming began when I worked closely with a software developer at my startup (Physeo). From the outside, it seemed like magic - a world which I found to be completely new, complex, creative, and interesting. I slowly began learning how to program in the evenings using free resources such a freecodecamp.org and codecademy.com. Eventually, I completed the freecodecamp tutorials on HTML, CSS, and javascript. For the past several months I have had a lot more free time and have focused heavily on improving my skills. I am currently most interested in continuing to progress as a developer while helping a company grow.
